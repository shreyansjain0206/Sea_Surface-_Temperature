Classification Model Results

Overview:

Here are the results of our classification model applied to the Sea Surface Temperature (SST) dataset. In this phase, we utilized a Random Forest Classifier to predict whether the sea surface temperature exceeds a specified threshold.

Model Training

We split the dataset into training and testing sets, with 80% used for training and 20% for testing. A Random Forest Classifier with 100 decision trees was employed to learn patterns from the training data.

Model Evaluation

The trained model demonstrated high performance, achieving an accuracy of 97%. Here's a breakdown of the classification metrics:

Above Threshold (Class 1): The model excels in identifying instances when the sea surface temperature exceeds the threshold, as indicated by high precision, recall, and F1-score.

Conclusion:

Our classification model successfully predicts sea surface temperature categories with a high level of accuracy.
